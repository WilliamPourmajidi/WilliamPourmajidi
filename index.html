I am an academic researcher and technology leader working at the intersection of cloud-native systems, software architecture, governance, observability, and AI-driven platforms.

Over more than two decades in the technology industry, I have held roles spanning network engineering, programming, enterprise architecture, and executive technology leadership. That industry background informs my research: I study cloud and AI systems as they are actually designed, deployed, operated, and governed in complex environments.

My Ph.D. research focused on cloud computing, cloud governance, and cloud-native applications. Building on that work, my current research investigates large-scale data analytics and AI systems as cloud-native solutions, with emphasis on architecture, reproducibility, observability, execution models, and governance.

Current Research Program

Architecting Governable Cloud-Native AI Systems

This work examines real-world cloud-native pipelines, storage strategies, and execution models used by large-scale data analytics and AI systems. The goal is to establish a systems-level foundation for observability, reproducibility, and governance in modern AI-driven platforms.

02Execution and storage models
03Observability and reproducibility
04Governance of AI systems

Cloud-Native Application Governance

Governance models for distributed applications built with containers, orchestration, service platforms, and cloud operational controls.

Data and AI Pipeline Architecture

Architecture patterns for analytics and AI workflows, including storage decisions, pipeline orchestration, and reproducible execution.

Observability and Evidence

Methods for making complex cloud-native systems auditable, explainable, measurable, and suitable for governance review.
